Vanilla Crème Brûlée

Crème brûlée is a classic French dessert known for its decadent simplicity. It consists of a rich and creamy custard base, infused with the sweet and aromatic essence of vanilla, which is delicately baked to achieve a smooth and velvety texture. The distinctive feature of crème brûlée is its caramelized sugar crust, created by sprinkling sugar on top of the custard and torching it until it forms a crisp, golden layer. This delightful dessert balances the contrast between the silky custard and the satisfying crack of the caramelized sugar, making it a favorite among dessert enthusiasts worldwide.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

30-35 min.

Chilling Time

4 hours or overnight

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Place two ramekins in a small baking dish.

step 2

If you're using a vanilla bean, split it in half lengthwise and scrape out the seeds. Add the seeds (or the vanilla extract) to the heavy cream in a saucepan. Heat the cream over medium heat, stirring occasionally until it's hot but not boiling. Remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly. If you used a vanilla bean, remove the bean pod.

step 3

In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks, sugar, and a pinch of salt together until the mixture is smooth and slightly thickened.

step 4

Slowly pour the warm cream into the egg mixture, whisking constantly to avoid curdling the eggs.

step 5

Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any lumps or bits of vanilla bean.

step 6

Pour the custard evenly into the two ramekins in the baking dish.

step 7

Fill the baking dish with hot water until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the ramekins to create a water bath.

step 8

Carefully place the baking dish with the ramekins into the preheated oven. Bake for about 30-35 minutes or until the edges are set, but the center is slightly jiggly. Keep an eye on them, as the exact time may vary.

step 9

Remove the ramekins from the water bath and let them cool to room temperature. Then, cover and refrigerate them for at least 4 hours or overnight.

step 10

Just before serving, sprinkle a thin, even layer of granulated sugar on top of each custard. Use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar until it forms a golden crust. If you don't have a torch, you can place the ramekins under a broiler for a minute or two, but watch them closely to avoid burning.

step 11

Let the crème brûlée sit for a minute or two to allow the sugar to harden, then serve and enjoy!

ingredients

1 cup heavy cream

1/2 vanilla bean or 1/2 tablespoon of pure vanilla extract

2 large egg yolks

1/4 cup granulated sugar

Pinch of salt
