Grilled Alaska Salmon

This recipe features Alaska salmon grilled on a cedar plank, enhanced with the refreshing combination of lemon and dill. The cedar plank infuses a subtle smokiness into the salmon, while the lemon and dill add a bright and herby touch. The result is a simple yet delicious dish that brings out the natural flavors of the salmon in a beautifully balanced way.

prep time

15 min

cook time

15-20 min

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (around 350-375°F or 175-190°C).

step 2

Brush both sides of the cedar plank with a bit of olive oil to prevent the salmon from sticking and to enhance the smoky flavor.

step 3

Place the cedar plank on the grill and close the lid. Allow it to heat for about 3-4 minutes until it starts to crackle and release its aroma.

step 4

While the cedar plank is heating, season the salmon fillets with salt and black pepper to taste.

step 5

Once the cedar plank is ready, place the lemon slices down then the seasoned salmon fillets on top of the lemon, skin-side down.

step 6

Lay the fresh dill sprigs over the salmon fillets.

step 7

Close the grill lid and let the salmon cook for approximately 15-20 minutes. The cooking time may vary depending on the thickness of the fillets, so check for doneness by ensuring the salmon flakes easily with a fork.

step 8

Keep a close eye on the cedar plank during grilling to ensure it doesn't catch fire. If it starts to flame, spritz it with a bit of water.

step 9

Carefully remove the cedar plank with the cooked salmon from the grill and let it rest for a minute before serving.

step 10

Serve the grilled salmon directly on the cedar plank, allowing the smoky aroma to infuse the dish. Discard the cedar plank after use.

ingredients

2 Alaska salmon fillets (6-8 ounces each) or 1 large fillet

1 untreated cedar plank (soaked in water for at least 1 hour)

1 lemon, thinly sliced

2-3 sprigs of fresh dill

Olive oil

Salt and black pepper to taste
