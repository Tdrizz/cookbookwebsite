Scrambled Eggs

These scrambled eggs are a testament to the art of breakfast perfection. Whisked to a silky consistency and cooked gently over low heat, they transform into fluffy, creamy, and delicate curds. The secret to their luxurious creaminess lies in the addition of crème fraîche, which enriches the texture. Finished with a sprinkle of chives or herbs for a burst of freshness, these scrambled eggs are a delightful and indulgent breakfast option that will leave you craving every bite.

prep time

5 min.

cook time

5 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk them until well combined. Add the crème fraîche (or heavy cream), salt, and pepper, and whisk again until the mixture is smooth and well incorporated.

step 2

Heat a non-stick skillet over low to medium-low heat. Add the butter and let it melt, coating the bottom of the skillet evenly.

step 3

Pour the egg mixture into the skillet. Allow it to sit undisturbed for a moment to start forming curds.

step 4

Using a rubber spatula, gently stir and fold the eggs as they begin to set. Be sure not to overcook; the eggs should remain soft and creamy.

step 5

Continue to cook and stir until the eggs are just set but still slightly runny, as they will continue to cook off the heat.

step 6

Remove the skillet from the heat just before the eggs are fully set. They should be slightly runny when you take them off the heat, as they will continue to cook from the residual heat.

step 7

Serve immediately, garnishing with chopped chives or herbs if desired.

ingredients

4 large eggs

2 tablespoons crème fraîche or heavy cream

Salt and pepper to taste

1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Chopped chives or herbs (optional, for garnish)
